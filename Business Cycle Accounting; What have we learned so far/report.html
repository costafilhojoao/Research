Introduction

Business Cycle Accounting is a preliminary data analysis technique. Its goals are (i) to isolate promising classes of models and (ii) to guide development of theory. Its two pillars are equivalence results and an accounting procedure. The first show how detailed models with frictions are equivalent to a prototype growth model with time-varying wedges. The second uses theory and data to measure wedges. This is done by estimating the stochastic process governing expectations. The estimated wedges are then fed back one at a time and in combinations to simulate "one-wedge-on" and "one-wedge-off" model economies. A measure which is indicative of how much of the observed aggregates are accounted for by each of the simulated economies is, inter alia, the correlation between observed data and data simulated in the "one-wedge-on" economies. We report these correlations in the following section.
